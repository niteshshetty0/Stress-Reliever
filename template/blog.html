Never Give Up Attitude

Joanne Rowling 31 July 1965 #TECH

The creator of “Harry Potter Series” has sold more than 400 million copies worldwide. Like many others, success didn’t come easy to her. She fought various battles in her life before becoming successful. As per sources, there was a time in her life when she was diagnosed with clinical depression and contemplated suicid. Her marriage had failed, and she was jobless with a dependent child. She was so poor that she couldn’t afford to support herself and her daughter. But, she never gave up on her dream to write. So in 1990, when she was on a train trip, the idea about a story of a young boy attending a school of wizardry came into her mind. In 1995, she finished the manuscript of her first book on an old manual typewriter. Her manuscript was rejected by twelve publishing houses. But she didn’t give up. And, today, she is United Kingdom’s best-selling living author, with an estimated fortune of £560 million, ranking her as the twelfth richest woman in the UK.

Nothing is Impossible

Oprah Gail Winfrey January 29, 1954 #TECH

A role model for young women everywhere, she proves that nothing is impossible. There are very few people who know how Oprah failed before becoming the richest African American woman in the world. Unlike many, Oprah wasn’t blessed with a peaceful childhood. From being abused at nine years old, Oprah got pregnant at 14. At an age when many of us were enjoying a carefree childhood, Oprah was struggling with problems that we can’t even imagine. But, she promised herself to not let her circumstances define her. She fought for a better future. And, with her persistence and determination, she became one of the most successful TV show anchors. Later, she took risks and started her own production company. Today, Winfrey is one of just 13 African American billionaires and an inspiration for women everywhere.

Believe in Your self

Walter Elias Disney December 5, 1901 #TECH

He was fired from the newspaper animation job for “not being creative enough.” And that was the turning point of Walt Disney’s life. Instead of moping around, Disney drew motivation from getting the boot. He started creating characters, one of which made him a fortune he couldn’t even dream of at that time. Today, Disney’s Mickey Mouse is so iconic that its popularity is more than almost 90% of humans that are alive. Think about that – a cartoon character so larger than life that it overshadowed real humans. Disney went on to win 22 academy awards (the most ever!) plus 4 honoraries, taking the tally to 26. He’s also the most oscar nominated person in history with a total of 59. From getting fired to becoming filthy rich for the same reason he was fired for, success stories like Disney’s should be taught in school.

Be limitless

Nick Vujicic 4 December 1982 #TECH

Nick Vujicic’s story is one of the most inspirational stories and motivational ones. The problems, your reasons, your excuses, you use to hide the pain of even giving it a try are just nothing in comparison to the life of this incredible man. Born with no limbs, i.e. no hands, no legs only a little flipper alike leg. Even then, Nick Vujicic lives a life which can only be imagined by most.He is a Painter, Swimmer, Skydiver, and a motivational speaker who inspires people with full capability.While struggling mentally, emotionally, and physically and Bullied at school, he tried to attempt suicide when he was just an age of 10 years. Now, Finally dealing with disability, Nick decided to become vocal about living without limbs and finding hope and inspiration in life.
